# Email Style Guide

## Tone
- Professional but warm
- Concise and direct
- Friendly without being overly casual

## Structure
1. **Greeting**: Use the sender's first name when known
2. **Acknowledgment**: Reference their message briefly
3. **Body**: Address their points directly
4. **Action items**: Be clear about next steps
5. **Sign-off**: Keep it professional

## Templates

### Acknowledging a request
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for reaching out about [topic].
>
> [Response]
>
> Let me know if you have any questions.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

### Scheduling a meeting
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'd be happy to discuss this further. Are you available [suggest 2-3 times]?
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

### Following up
> Hi [Name],
>
> Just wanted to follow up on my previous email about [topic].
>
> [Brief context or question]
>
> Thanks,
> [Your name]

## Sign-offs
- **Formal**: Best regards, Kind regards
- **Standard**: Best, Thanks
- **Casual** (if appropriate): Cheers

## Things to avoid
- All caps (comes across as shouting)
- Excessive exclamation marks
- Overly long paragraphs
- Jargon the recipient might not understand
